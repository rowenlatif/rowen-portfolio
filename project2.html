Kappa Theta Pi App

The KTP Pledge App is a mobile prototype designed to streamline the pledge experience for Kappa Theta Pi, a professional tech fraternity. Built with a retro-tech aesthetic, the app helps pledges stay organized by centralizing tasks, events, and communication in one place. Features like secure login, automated reminders, a social directory, and a gamified leaderboard make the process more intuitive, engaging, and easy to manage.

UX/UI Design | Mobile | User-Centered Design

2025

Project Purpose

THE PROBLEM

Kappa Theta Pi (KTP), a professional tech fraternity I’m a part of, needed a more efficient way to help pledges stay on top of their tasks, deadlines, and events. The old system was pieced together with spreadsheets, emails, and word-of-mouth reminders; it was confusing for new members and hard to manage for Pledge Masters. It was clear the process needed a digital upgrade that could centralize everything while still feeling approachable and fun.

THE SOLUTION

I designed a mobile app prototype for KTP pledges, using a retro-tech theme. The app includes a secure login system tied to university emails. Once inside, users get a personalized dashboard that shows their progress, upcoming events, and key requirements at a glance. I also designed features like a customizable profile, a task tracker to sort and prioritize checklist items, and a notification system that sends automatic reminders. To keep things fun, I added a leaderboard to gamify task completion. There's also a calendar for events and a directory where pledges can track who they've met in the fraternity. I worked closely with KTP leadership and used an iterative design process to make sure the features actually support pledges and simplify their experience.

THE TAKEAWAY

Working on this app gave me experience designing for a very specific, real-world user base. I learned a lot about balancing utility with personality, and how to design features that feel genuinely helpful without overwhelming the user. It was also a great opportunity to practice research-driven design, as I worked closely with PMs to understand what pledges actually need during the process. The result was a thoughtful, scalable system that could really improve the pledge experience from day one.
