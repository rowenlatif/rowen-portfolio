inkling

inkling is a reading companion app designed for emotionally intuitive readers who love exploring new books but don’t always finish them. Focused on first chapters, inkling helps users log their initial impressions, capturing how a book made them feel before diving deeper. Through mood tagging, quote-saving, and a soft social layer, inkling transforms book discovery into a reflective, low-pressure experience. With a minimal, literary-inspired aesthetic and a quiet, reader-first design philosophy, the app centers emotional resonance over completion or ratings.

Project Purpose

THE PROBLEM

Traditional book platforms often focus on tracking progress or assigning ratings, but not every book needs to be finished to make an impact. Many readers want a way to explore more intentionally, without the pressure to complete every title. There was no dedicated space to log emotional responses, save standout first lines, or track a book's vibe from the very beginning.

THE SOLUTION

I designed inkling as a gentle alternative to performance-based reading apps. The MVP includes first-chapter logging, mood and vibe tagging, quote-saving, and a personal library organized by emotional resonance rather than completion status. The interface is soft and clean, inspired by vintage notebooks and subtle ink textures, with features that prioritize reflection over review. A soft social layer enables connection without the noise of traditional comment threads; only first-chapter impressions are visible. Every interaction is designed to feel quiet, personal, and intuitive.

THE TAKEAWAY

inkling helped me dive deep into designing for emotion, showing how thoughtful interaction and branding can reshape even familiar experiences like reading. I learned how to blend UX systems with a strong emotional concept, build from values like “quiet innovation”, and prioritize user feelings without sacrificing structure or clarity. This project reinforced how powerful gentle design can be, especially when paired with a mission to honor beginnings over endings.
