Starborne

Starborne is a two-player, card-based battle game designed around a celestial theme, blending whimsical sci-fi aesthetics with strategic gameplay. Created collaboratively, the game invites players to draw from 30 unique cards featuring celestial entities such as planets, moons, constellations, and more and use them to attack, defend, and outmaneuver their opponent in a bid to reduce their Starlife to zero.

Project Purpose

THE PROBLEM

We set out to design a physical game that was visually engaging, strategically fun, and thematically cohesive, without relying on digital interfaces. Our biggest challenge was crafting a rule system that felt intuitive and smooth while accounting for physical constraints like card size, game balance, and the complexity of interactions.

THE SOLUTION

We developed an original 2-player card game from the ground up, including rules, game modes, card designs, and play mechanics. Each card was hand-illustrated and built with stats like health, attack power, and unique abilities. We prototyped and refined through sketching, digital design (Procreate), and multiple test prints using 80lb cardstock. The final set included 30 playable cards, a board, a box, and optional visual health counters ("Starlives"). Our design process emphasized visual storytelling and collaborative iteration.

THE TAKEAWAY

Starborne taught me how to manage a full creative production pipeline, from concept to prototyping to presentation. I learned how to design within a group while balancing t hematic unity with individual artistic expression. It also reinforced the value of playtesting and iteration, as we adapted rules and mechanics based on real feedback.
